# Becomming a HAM
I've been interested in radio for a while. I have some hobby projects that I'd planned to write about, 
but only ever got as far as a [draft](https://github.com/tlofreso/tlofreso.github.io/blob/main/docs/posts/drafts/tracking-flights-overhead.md). 
There are a few projects I'd like to do that will require me to be a licensed operator. This is a collection of the 
resources I found most useful throughout the process.

## My Motivation
People have [many reasons](https://www.arrl.org/why-i-love-it) to get their license, and love to talk about them. My reasons are as follows:

 - Stay sharp with the basics (ohms law, basic electronics, understanding rf)
 - Buy, build, and use cool tech (maker space, awesome antennas, awesome masts)
 - Level up SDR ()
 - Stay informed (weather data, local police / ems)
 - Get involved in the local community (emergency response, comms setup)

## My Goal
Become a part of the HAM community, which appears to be incredibly welcoming... with a growing desire to encourage young folks to become 
HAMs as the hobby has been aging steadily for decades. To this end, I intend to earn the Tenchnical, and Genral class licenses. Here's 
an overview of the available [license levels](https://www.arrl.org/ham-radio-licenses).

## Resources
Studying has been straight forward. If you have a bsic understanding of electricity, and know your orders of magnitude... you're welll on your way. 
Here are the items I found most useful for study:

 - [ARRL HAM Tech License Manual](https://home.arrl.org/action/Store/Product-Details/productId/2003373064)
 - [ARRL HAM General License Manual](https://home.arrl.org/action/Store/Product-Details/productId/2009476073)
 - [HAMStudy App](https://hamstudy.org/appstore)

The app specifically is fantastic. It will intelligently feed you questions, and has great explanations for questions you're having trouble with. 
For example, some of the exam questions rely basically on memorization.
